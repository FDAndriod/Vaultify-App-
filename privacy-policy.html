1. Who we are

Vaultify is an Android app that provides a secure, PIN‑protected vault to hide and organize private files on your device. The app is offered by [YOUR DEVELOPER / COMPANY NAME] ("we"). For privacy-related questions, contact us at: [YOUR_CONTACT_EMAIL].

2. Information we collect and how we use it

2.1 Data stored only on your device (we do not collect this)

  • Vault content: Photos, videos, audio, and documents you add to the vault are stored only on your device. We do not upload, access, or collect this content.
  • PIN and security answers: Your PIN and security question/answer are processed and stored only on your device. We do not have access to them.
  • App preferences: Settings such as disguise icon choice and fingerprint enable/disable are stored locally on your device.

2.2 Analytics and crash reporting (optional / automatic)

To improve the app and fix crashes, we may use:

  • Google Firebase Analytics – to understand how the app is used (e.g. screens opened, actions taken). This may include device-related information (e.g. device model, OS version) and usage events. No vault content or PIN is sent.
  • Google Firebase Crashlytics – to receive crash reports and technical diagnostics. Again, no vault content or PIN is included.

You can limit or opt out of analytics via your device or Google account settings where applicable. Crash data helps us fix bugs and is processed in line with Google's policies.

2.3 Permissions we use

  • Storage / Media (photos, videos, files): To add files to your vault and, if you choose, to remove them from the device gallery after adding to the vault. We only access files you explicitly select.
  • Camera: To take photos for the vault or for the in‑app document/OCR scanning feature.
  • Biometric (fingerprint): To let you unlock the app with fingerprint when you enable this option. Biometric data is handled by your device's system; we do not collect or store it.

We do not use these permissions for anything other than the features described above.

2.4 Advertising and consent (Google AdMob and partners)

Vaultify is free with advertising. To show ads, measure delivery, and comply with regional requirements, we use:

  • Google Mobile Ads (AdMob) – to display banner and full-screen (interstitial) ads.
  • Ad mediation – Google may route ad requests to third-party ad networks (for example Meta Audience Network when enabled in our mediation setup). Those partners may process data under their own policies and Google’s requirements for participating networks.
  • Google User Messaging Platform (UMP) – where required or offered, to collect or record consent for ads and related processing (for example in the EEA, UK, and Switzerland) and to manage certain ad choices. UMP may store consent-related information on your device and with Google’s consent tools as described in Google’s documentation.

Types of data that ad technologies may process (not your vault file contents): identifiers such as Advertising ID where available, IP address (which may be used to infer approximate location), device and app technical information, and ad interaction data (for example impressions, clicks, and limited conversion-related signals). We do not pass your vault photos, videos, documents, PIN, or security answers to ad networks for ad targeting; ad SDKs operate separately from your stored vault files.

Network access: The app uses internet connectivity to load ads, present consent messages where applicable, and to send the Firebase Analytics and Crashlytics events described in section 2.2.

For more on Google’s requirements for apps that show ads, see Google Play’s advertising and monetization policy.

3. Data sharing and selling

We do not sell your personal data. We do not share your vault content, PIN, or security answers with third parties in the ordinary sense of “sharing your files,” because that information is intended to remain on your device under your control.

Service providers and advertising: Like many apps, we rely on Google and, where ads are shown, Google’s advertising partners (including mediation partners such as Meta, when configured) to serve and measure ads and to operate Firebase Analytics and Crashlytics. Those companies process certain data on their own systems under their privacy policies and industry frameworks (for example IAB transparency and consent mechanisms where applicable). This processing is separate from your vault file storage and is described in more detail in sections 2.2 and 2.4.

4. Data retention and deletion

  • On your device: Vault content and app data remain on your device until you delete them or uninstall the app. You can remove items from the vault or unhide them back to your device using the app.
  • Analytics/crash data: Retained according to Google Firebase's retention settings (typically as described in Google's privacy policy). We do not retain your vault content or PIN anywhere on our servers.
  • Advertising-related data: Retention and further processing are governed primarily by Google and ad / mediation partners' policies, your device and Google account settings (including reset advertising ID and ad personalization controls), and any consent recorded through UMP where applicable.

5. Security

We use standard security practices: vault content and access are protected by your PIN (and optional fingerprint) and stored on your device. You are responsible for keeping your PIN and device secure. We do not transmit or store your vault content on our servers.
